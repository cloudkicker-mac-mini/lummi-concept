News & Updates

Important announcements and information for LNHA residents and applicants

February 11, 2026

30-Day Notice

Important update regarding notice requirements for LNHA residents. All tenants must provide proper 30-day written notice when planning to vacate a rental unit. This notice should be submitted to the LNHA office and must include your intended move-out date.

What You Need to Know:

  • Notice must be submitted in writing
  • Minimum 30 days before your intended move-out date
  • Submit to LNHA main office or contact your Housing Resident Advocate

Questions? Contact your Housing Resident Advocate or call (360) 312-8407.

December 18, 2025

MAC Gym Holiday Hours

The MAC Gym will observe modified hours during the holiday season to allow our staff time with their families. Please plan accordingly and contact the facility for specific holiday closures.

For current hours and holiday schedule information, please call the main office at (360) 312-8407.

December 18, 2025

Kwina Village Laundry Update

Kwina Village laundry facilities have new hours and updated information for residents. The laundry room is available to all Kwina Village residents and has been updated with improved equipment.

Kwina Village residents can contact their Housing Resident Advocate for current hours and access information.

November 26, 2025

Important Notice: Flushable Wipes

Do not flush "flushable" wipes down toilets. Despite marketing claims, these wipes do not break down like toilet paper and cause serious plumbing problems and backups in our housing units.

What This Means for Residents:

  • Only flush toilet paper — nothing else
  • Dispose of all wipes (even "flushable" ones) in the trash
  • Help prevent costly plumbing repairs and backups
  • Report any plumbing issues immediately to Maintenance

Maintenance issues? Contact Christina Solomon, Maintenance Supervisor, at (360) 312-8407 x2431.

November 7, 2025

HUD IHBG Community Participation

The Lummi Nation Housing Authority invites community members to participate in the planning process for the Indian Housing Block Grant (IHBG) program. Your input helps shape housing priorities and programs that serve our community.

What is IHBG?

The Indian Housing Block Grant is federal funding from the Department of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) that supports affordable housing development, rehabilitation, and housing services for tribal members.

Want to participate? Contact Uriah Guillory, Housing Community Planner/Grant Writer, at (360) 312-8407 x2403 to learn about upcoming community input sessions.

Questions or Concerns?

Our team is here to help. Contact us for information about programs, maintenance, or any housing-related questions.
