Housing Services

Our Programs

Comprehensive housing support for Lummi tribal members and families

Low-Income Rental Program

Over 300 low-income rental homes (1-5 bedrooms) located at Kwina Village, Tiopi Loop, Balch Road, Smokehouse Village, and the Mackenzie area. These homes are available for qualified Lummi Tribal members and their families.

Locations:

  • Kwina Village
  • Tiopi Loop
  • Balch Road
  • Smokehouse Village
  • Mackenzie area

Required Forms:

  • Housing Application
  • Waiting List Update (must update annually)

Low-Income Rental Assistance

Financial assistance programs to help low-income tribal members secure and maintain housing.

Emergency Housing Assistance

Short-term emergency housing support for low-income tribal members facing housing crises.

First and Last Month's Rent

LNHA pays initial occupancy costs to help tribal members move into housing.

Student Rental Assistance

Financial support for full-time students attending an accredited college or university.

Required Forms:

  • First and Last Month's Rent Application
  • Student Rental Assistance Application
View Application Forms →

Home Repairs & Rehabilitation

Assistance programs for non-HUD home repairs and rehabilitation.

Home Repairs Assistance (Non-HUD)

Financial assistance for repairs to interior, exterior, and roof of non-HUD homes.

Home Repair Loans (Over-Income)

Loan program available for over-income applicants who need home repair assistance.

Requirements:

  • Lummi enrollment
  • Homeowner's insurance
  • Income verification for all household members 18+
  • Proof of ownership (title/deed)
  • Veteran card (if applicable)

Required Forms:

  • Repair and Rehab Application
  • Repair and Rehab Update
  • Emergency Application
View Application Forms →

Supportive Housing

Specialized housing programs providing wraparound services and supportive environments.

Sche'lang'en Village Transformational Wrap-Around Program

Comprehensive wraparound services for tribal members and families needing additional support.

Sche'lang'en Crossroads Housing (SCH)

Well-briety living environment providing supportive housing for tribal members coming out of treatment.

Contact Us for More Information

Home Ownership

Programs to support homeownership opportunities for qualified enrolled Lummi Tribal members.

Down Payment Assistance Program

Financial assistance for down payments to help qualified enrolled Lummi Tribal members purchase homes.

New Construction (Tribal Land)

Homeownership opportunities available as new homes are constructed on tribal land.
