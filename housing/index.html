Since 2006

Housing Families, Building Communities

The Lummi Nation Housing Authority supports tribal members by increasing opportunities for housing that is decent, safe, sanitary, and affordable, and to promote opportunities for homeownership. Over 350 low-income rental and homeownership units developed since 2006.

What We Offer

Our Programs

Low-Income Rental Program

Over 300 rental homes (1-5 bedrooms) at Kwina Village, Tiopi Loop, Balch Road, Smokehouse Village, and Mackenzie area for qualified Lummi tribal members and families.

Rental Assistance

Emergency housing assistance, first and last month's rent support, and student rental assistance for full-time college students.

Home Repairs & Rehabilitation

Home repair assistance for non-HUD homes including interior, exterior, and roof repairs. Loans available for over-income applicants.

Supportive Housing

Sche'lang'en Village wraparound program and Crossroads Housing well-briety living environment for members coming out of treatment.

Home Ownership

Down payment assistance and new construction on tribal land for qualified enrolled Lummi tribal members.
