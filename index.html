𓆟 Protecting Our Waters

Joint Initiative

Salmon Need Water

A partnership between the Lummi Nation and Nooksack Tribe to protect water rights in Whatcom County. Today we face diminishing salmon runs, a growing population, and a changing climate. Adjudication — a formal court judgment of water claims based on the facts — is the only fair way to resolve water disputes.

We are neighbors in this beautiful place. We are working to leave salmon in our streams and rivers for our children, grandchildren, and future generations.

Visit SalmonNeedWater.org

Self-Governance

Our Government

The Lummi Indian Business Council is the elected governing body of the Lummi Nation. Through the Council, General Manager's Office, and departments spanning health, education, natural resources, public safety, and economic development, we serve our community with sovereignty and purpose.

As one of South King County's largest employers, the Lummi Nation supports 3,300 direct jobs and contributes hundreds of millions of dollars to the regional economy.
