Our Mission

The mission of the Lummi Tribal Health Center is to raise the health status of the Lummi people and other American Indians and Alaskan Natives to the highest possible level.

We provide comprehensive health care including outpatient, medical, dental, pharmacy, preventative, physical therapy, psychiatry, rheumatology, and public health services. Our commitment is to deliver culturally sensitive, patient-centered care that honors the traditions and needs of our community.

Health & Family Services Commission

The Lummi Health & Family Services Commission consists of nine tribal members appointed by the Lummi Indian Business Council. The Commission governs and regulates operational policies for the Health Center and Family Services Program.

Governance & Advocacy

The Commission serves as the governing body for the Health Center, ensuring that services meet the needs of our community while maintaining the highest standards of care. As advocates, they work to preserve the Federal Government's Trust Obligation to provide health care to Indian people.

This obligation, established under the Point Elliott Treaty of 1855, recognizes the government's duty to support the health and wellbeing of tribal nations. The Commission ensures this commitment is honored and that our community receives the care we are owed.

Accredited

Quality Healthcare You Can Trust

The Lummi Tribal Health Center is accredited by the Accreditation Association for Ambulatory Health Care Inc. (AAAHC). This accreditation reflects our commitment to maintaining the highest standards in patient safety, care quality, and operational excellence.

AAAHC accreditation demonstrates that we meet rigorous national standards for healthcare quality. Our team undergoes regular evaluations to ensure we continue providing the best possible care to our community.

Two Types of Care

Direct Care

Healthcare services provided directly at the Lummi Tribal Health Center by our medical, dental, pharmacy, behavioral health, and therapy staff. This includes all primary care, preventative services, and many specialty services available on-site.

Purchase & Referred Care (PRC)

Funds designated to purchase approved health care services that are not provided at the Lummi Tribal Health Center. PRC covers specialty care, advanced diagnostics, and treatments requiring external facilities.

Important: PRC operates as the Payor of Last Resort. Eligible patients must apply for alternate insurance coverage (Medicaid, Medicare, state programs) before PRC can be used.
