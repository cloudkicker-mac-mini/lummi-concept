Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to common questions about our services, eligibility, and scheduling.

Who is eligible for services at the Health Center?

Services are available to individuals enrolled in a federally recognized tribe or those who can provide proof of Indian descent (1st or 2nd degree). Non-Native individuals may be eligible in certain situations, such as being pregnant with an eligible patient's child, or needing care to control a public health threat. Learn more about eligibility requirements.

How do I schedule an appointment?

You can schedule appointments by calling our main office at (360) 384-0464 during business hours. Existing patients can also use MyChart to schedule, reschedule, or cancel appointments online. Same-day appointments are available for urgent needs—call to check availability.

What is MyChart and how do I access it?

MyChart is an online patient portal that gives you 24/7 access to your medical records, lab results, medications, and appointment scheduling. You can message your provider, request prescription refills, and more. Ask at patient registration or call us to set up your MyChart account. Sign in to MyChart.

How does the pharmacy work?

The Health Center pharmacy provides prescription medications for eligible direct care patients. Our pharmacists are available to answer questions about your medications, provide counseling, and help manage refills. Pharmacy hours are Monday through Friday, 8:30 AM to 5 PM. Call (360) 384-0464 for prescription refills or questions.

What is Purchase & Referred Care (PRC)?

PRC is funding for approved healthcare services that are not provided at the Health Center, such as specialty care or advanced diagnostics. PRC operates as the Payor of Last Resort, which means eligible patients must apply for alternate insurance coverage (Medicaid, Medicare, state programs) before PRC funds can be used. Contact the PRC office at the Health Center for more information.

Is transportation assistance available?

Yes. The Health Center offers transportation assistance for eligible patients to help access medical appointments and healthcare services. Contact our transportation office to learn about eligibility and how to arrange a ride.

What should I do if I need care after hours?

For urgent medical needs after regular clinic hours, call our after-hours line at (360) 715-2447. For life-threatening emergencies, always call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room.

What documents do I need to register as a new patient?

New patients should bring: registration form (available at the front desk), tribal enrollment documentation, birth certificate (for patients under 18), proof of residency, and insurance coverage information if applicable. View the full list of required documents.

Do I need health insurance to receive care?

Health insurance is not required to receive direct care services at the Health Center if you meet eligibility requirements. However, you are encouraged to apply for available insurance programs (Medicaid, Medicare, etc.), especially if you may need Purchase & Referred Care (PRC) services in the future. Our staff can help you with health benefits sign-up.

Does the Health Center provide dental services?

Yes. We offer comprehensive dental care including preventative, restorative, emergency, and specialty services such as dental implants, dentures, root canals, oral surgery, orthodontics, and sedation dentistry. Dental hours are Monday through Friday, 8 AM to 4:30 PM. Learn more about dental services.

Are mental health services available?

Yes. Our Behavioral Health department provides individual, couples, and family mental health therapy. We also offer psychiatry services for medication management. Call the Health Center to schedule an appointment or ask your primary care provider for a referral.

Still Have Questions?

If you didn't find the answer you're looking for, please don't hesitate to contact us. We're here to help you access the care you need.
