New Patients

Registration & Scheduling

Everything you need to know about becoming a patient at Lummi Tribal Health Center.

Eligibility Requirements

To receive services at the Lummi Tribal Health Center, patients must meet one of the following criteria:

Enrolled Tribal Member

Enrolled in a federally recognized tribe with documentation of enrollment.

Proof of Indian Descent

1st or 2nd degree Indian descent with documentation (Certificate of Indian Blood or tribal documentation).

Non-Tribal Eligibility Exceptions

In certain situations, non-Native individuals may be eligible for specific services:

  • Pregnant with an eligible patient: Non-Native individuals pregnant with an eligible patient's child may receive care during pregnancy and for a limited period after birth.
  • Public health threat control: Services may be provided when necessary to control a public health threat to the community.
  • Significant other/household member: Non-Native individuals who are significant others or household members of eligible patients may be eligible for substance use disorder treatment and assistance with health benefits sign-up.

Required Documents

Please bring the following documents when registering as a new patient:

  • Registration form — Available at the front desk or by calling (360) 384-0464
  • Tribal enrollment documentation — Enrollment card or certificate from your tribe
  • Birth certificate — Required for patients under 18 years of age
  • Proof of residency — Utility bill, lease agreement, or other documentation showing current address
  • Insurance coverage information — If you have Medicare, Medicaid, private insurance, or other coverage

Scheduling Appointments

By Phone

Call our main office during business hours to schedule an appointment with one of our providers.

MyChart Online

Existing patients can schedule, reschedule, and manage appointments 24/7 through MyChart.

Walk-In/Same-Day

Same-day appointments are available for urgent needs. Call ahead or check in at patient registration to inquire about availability.

Important:

All patients must check in with patient registration at each visit, even if you have an appointment scheduled. This ensures your records are up-to-date and helps us serve you efficiently.
